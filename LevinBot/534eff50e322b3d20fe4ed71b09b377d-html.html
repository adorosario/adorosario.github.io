When the central nervous system is forming in an embryonic frog, groups of surface cells become hyperpolarized relative to their neighbors. In this cover image, three 
of these groups have been pseudocolored on the right half of the embryo. The descendants of those cell groups are shown in the same colors in the tadpole face. This 
bioelectric pre-pattern is regulated by the the activity of Kir2.1, an inwardly rectifying potassium channel. Micrographs and pseudocoloring by Dany Spencer Adams, 
overall design by Jeremy Guay of Peregrine Crative LLC. (See Adams et al. pp 3245–3270).
